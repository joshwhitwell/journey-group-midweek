Journey Group Midweek

Series One

Inspire, Educate, Challenge

01/10/2024–02/28/2024

Description

Each person is assigned to a group consisting of 2-3 cross-studio collaborators. Each group is assigned a date on which they will lead BOTH Midweek AND Doodling (including Doodling Vibes®, i.e., music selection). The format of the activity for that week (e.g., presentation, workshop, field trip, etc.) is up to the group, but should be thematically related to one of the prompts below. Official programming will begin at 12:15pm each week; the exact breakdown between Midweek and Doodling is flexible, but sufficient time should be reserved for the latter (~20 minutes).

Topics

  • Inspire

    Showcase a design, person, or idea that you find inspiring. A given group does not have to agree on a single source of inspiration; it could synthesize topics across categories, or alternatively compare and contrast within a category.

  • Educate

    Teach us about a current trend(s) that you see emerging in your professional work or in society. Help us to contextualize and to think better about this trend across modalities (for instance as designers, as a community, as individuals).

  • Challenge

    Pose a design problem(s) that we workshop collectively or in small groups. The problem could be a concrete/technical challenge (e.g., redesign a local business’s brand, or reimagine the 418 parking lot mural), or more abstract (e.g., how can we incorporate more natural space into our urban environment?).

Series Two

Tenable

03/13/2024–09/11/2024

Description

Tenable is a multi-part, group series that investigates issues of sustainability in our personal, professional, and communal lives. Participants are organized into small teams and assigned a sub-topic, which they will explore together over the course of several months. The series is broken into three phases: 1) Define Problem; 2) Research Solutions; 3) Present Findings. Each group leads once during each phase, i.e., each group will first define their problem, then each group will lead a reseach activity, then each group will present their findings. The format for a given week for a given phase is up to the group (e.g., slideshow presentation, Q&A, panel, workshop, film, field trip, guest lecture, etc.), but should align with the goal for that phase.
